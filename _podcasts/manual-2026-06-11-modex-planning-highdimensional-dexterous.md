## Paper

**MoDex: Planning High-Dimensional Dexterous Control via Learning Neural Internal Models** — Tong Wu, Shoujie Li, Chuqiao Lyu et al.

## Summary Card

### Motivation
Controlling high-dimensional dexterous hands is a longstanding challenge in robotics, yet humans perform such tasks effortlessly. This work aims to bridge this gap by leveraging the concept of internal models from human motor control to enable efficient learning and planning for dexterous manipulation.

### Method
MoDex proposes a framework with neural networks that learn the dynamical characteristics of dexterous hands, coupled with a bidirectional planning approach. Key innovations include integrating an external model for in-hand object manipulation and a large language model for gesture generation, enabling both training and planning efficiency across simulation and real-world settings.

### Key Results
Extensive experiments demonstrate that MoDex achieves data efficiency in learning new tasks and transferability between different dexterous manipulation tasks. The framework successfully generates diverse gestures and manipulates objects in both simulation and real-world environments, validating its versatility.

### Takeaways
MoDex shows that learning neural internal models can make high-dimensional dexterous control more tractable and sample-efficient. This approach bridges the gap between human-like dexterity and robotic systems, with potential applications in assistive robotics, teleoperation, and human-robot interaction.
